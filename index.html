The Cycle Double Cover Theorem

The Proof Reduction Pipeline

1

General Graph to Cubic Expansion

Any loopless bridgeless multigraph can be expanded into a 3-regular (cubic) graph by expanding each vertex of degree d > 3 into a ring of d vertices connected by rings of helper edges, preserving bridgelessness.

2

Jaeger-Kilpatrick 8-Flow Theorem

We show that every finite bridgeless multigraph has a nowhere-zero F₂³-flow (an 8-flow). The proof inducts on the size of the vertex set, reducing 2-cuts by edge contraction.

3

Tree Packing & Nash-Williams

We pack three disjoint spanning-connected trees in the doubled graph. The core of this step is proving Diestel's exchange lemma, which replaces Kaiser's partition sequences in our formalization.

4

Flow Projection & List Assembly

An 8-flow gives three even edge covers whose symmetric difference properties cover each edge exactly twice. Finally, these even edge sets are decomposed into minimal cycles, constructing the Cycle Double Cover list.
